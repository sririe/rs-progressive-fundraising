﻿Working Session - Redstamp + Progressive Fundraising (Digital Gift Card Fulfilment Workflow Mapping) (In Office)
🧾 Section 1: Verbatim Notes (Structured, No Interpretation)
General Process Notes
* High attention to detail required for the digital fulfillment process

* Password is invoice number, no letters, no dashes

* Month, day reversal issues, no customer appearing, PowerShell script breaks

* Claim code, serial number, customer and denomination

* Client gets an Excel file per merchant per order

* If five merchants, they get five files

* They receive a zip or URL file as options

* They provide an encrypted password Excel file to the clients

* They have to verify manually the password each time of each Excel file or PDF

* So many manual steps, very disorganized

* Petro-Canada, they have old inventory issues, but not really an issue anymore, they just send the new inventory

Questions (Captured As-Is)
   * Are there minimum orders or thresholds for digital cards?

   * Is there always an assumption that the card numbers are valid?

   * Is there any way of validating that the card numbers are valid before sending them to the customer?

   * How many digital orders a day?

   * Would the random entries be entered manually on QuickBooks Online by a Progressive employee?

   * Why do clients get a PDF for Amazon?

   * Is the Walmart script pinging the internet to get the numbers or pins?

Observations (Captured As-Is)
      * Consolidate an order to simplify the invoice

      * Website upload to match and verify against order would provide check and validation in that process

      * How do we make the client who receives the card numbers’ lives easier?

      * Give them the ability to validate balances, the usage of the cards, ability to send out in different ways so they know that the customer uses the cards

      * Security posture, risk tolerance, present to Doug to choose own adventure

      * Run books for clients on how to do things would make their lives easier

      * No checks or validations built into PowerShell
